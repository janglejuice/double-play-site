# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary guests: Cubs fans and baseball travelers making a pilgrimage to Wrigley Field. Secondary: Chicago leisure travelers, groups of friends or families (who may book 2 or all 3 units together), and concert/event attendees at Wrigley and nearby venues.

Context: They're planning an experience, not just securing a bed. They're excited. They may be comparing us to a hotel or platform listing. They want to feel confident they're booking from a real, trustworthy host who knows the neighborhood cold.

## Product Purpose

Direct booking site for 3 x 2bd/1ba vacation rental apartments at 3601 N Sheffield Ave, Chicago — directly across from Wrigley Field. Goal: capture bookings without Airbnb/VRBO fees, build an owned guest relationship, and rank on Google Vacation Rentals.

Success = guests who feel they made the right call before they even check in. A repeat guest and a Google review are the ideal outcomes.

## Brand Personality

Local, Genuine, Spirited.

A real host who loves Wrigleyville and gets genuinely excited about game day. Personal and specific, not corporate and vague. Warm but not cloying. Knows the neighborhood the way a longtime resident does, not the way a travel blogger does.

Voice: first person plural ("we," "our"). Opinionated recommendations. Honest about what the units are and aren't. Enthusiastic about the location without resorting to superlatives.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate hotel chains** — stiff, transactional, no personality, interchangeable rooms
- **Cheap vacation rental sites** — cluttered layouts, discount-badge energy, low-trust feel
- **Overly trendy startup sites** — gradient blobs, glassmorphism, tech-bro aesthetics that have nothing to do with hospitality

Also not: Airbnb/VRBO generic listing energy (faceless, card-grid, zero host voice).

## Design Principles

1. **Local knowledge wins.** Be specific. "Directly across from Wrigley Field" beats "near Wrigley." A walkable block list beats a map pin. Real neighborhood knowledge earns trust that generic copy never can.

2. **Host voice, not platform voice.** "We" throughout. Recommendations, tips, opinions. Guests are booking from a person, not checking out of a catalog. Every page should feel like a message from someone who lives there.

3. **Experience first, booking second.** Show guests what staying here feels like before asking them to pick dates. Excitement drives conversion more than form fields.

4. **Trust through specificity.** Real photos, real reviews, real building layout. No vague claims ("steps away," "vibrant neighborhood"). If you can count the steps, count them.

5. **Wrigleyville character without the cliche.** The ivy, the marquee, the rooftops are real and worth showing — but the neighborhood is more than a postcard. Lean into what a longtime resident knows, not what a tourist brochure says.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Strong color contrast on all text. Readable body type (Manrope, comfortable sizing). No reliance on color alone for meaning. Reduced motion: respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animations.
