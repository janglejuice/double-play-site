Frequently asked questions

Can we actually see the game from inside the apartment?

You can see the Wrigley Field marquee and the scoreboard from the living room windows — and on a quiet night you can hear the crowd clearly. You can't see the playing field directly (the ballpark walls block that), but you know exactly when something happens. A lot of guests watch the first few innings from the couch before heading over.

Is parking available?

There is no dedicated parking with the unit, and we genuinely recommend not driving. Wrigleyville parking on game days runs $40–60. The Addison Red Line is a 3-minute walk and runs 24/7. A 3-day CTA Ventra pass is $15 and gets you everywhere in Chicago. We also have a Divvy bike share station directly outside the building.

Do we need Cubs tickets to stay here?

Absolutely not. Many of our guests come for the neighborhood, not a specific game. The unit is great any time of year — Gallagher Way hosts free events year-round, the Lakefront Trail is 15 minutes east, and the Red Line puts you downtown in 15 minutes. That said, if you want tickets, text us before you book — we sometimes have leads on good seats.

What's the noise level like on game days?

Sheffield Avenue gets lively on game days — that's part of the experience. The apartment has double-pane windows which help significantly. If noise is a concern, our Field View Loft on the 3rd floor is the quietest unit in the building. Slugger's Suite is for guests who want to be in the middle of it, not away from it.

Can we host a group larger than 4 people?

The unit comfortably sleeps 4 (two queens) and we max out at 4 guests. If you're a larger group, we have two other units in the same building — the Bleacher Balcony Flat and the Field View Loft. Groups of 8–12 often book all three. Text us and we'll work something out.
