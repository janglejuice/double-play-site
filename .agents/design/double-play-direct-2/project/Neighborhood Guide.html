What It's Actually Like

The neighborhood that grew up around a ballpark.

Wrigleyville is the Chicago neighborhood that grew up around Wrigley Field, the second-oldest ballpark in America. On a game day, the streets fill with red and blue jerseys two hours before first pitch and stay loud until last call. Murphy's Bleachers spills onto Sheffield, the marquee at Clark and Addison glows, and the "Go Cubs Go" chant rolls out of every bar.

"But what surprises most of our guests is how great it is on non-game days."

The restaurants on Clark Street are packed with locals year-round. Gallagher Way, the plaza next to the ballpark, hosts free concerts, yoga classes, outdoor movies, and a German Christmas market in winter. The Lakefront Trail is a 15-minute walk east.

And we're a 15-minute train ride from downtown Chicago, the Art Institute, Lake Michigan's beaches, and the Magnificent Mile. You don't need a rental car. You don't need to plan. You just need to walk out the front door.

01 — Bars & Pregame

Drink like a local.

These are the bars we actually go to — not just the ones tourists find on their own.

photo · Murphy's Bleachers patio
Host Pick
2 min walk $$$

Murphy's Bleachers

The legendary outdoor bar right across from the bleacher entrance. On game days it's the only place to be — order a beer before you even think about going inside.

Host Tip
Get there 90 minutes early on game days. The patio fills up fast and the vibe before first pitch is completely unmatched.
photo · Sluggers batting cages
4 min walk $$$

Sluggers World Class Sports Bar

Two floors, batting cages upstairs, 20+ TVs everywhere. Exactly what it sounds like — and better than you'd expect. Great for large groups.

Host Tip
The batting cages on the upper level are genuinely fun and never as crowded as the main bar — even during games.
photo · Gman Tavern interior
8 min walk $$$

Gman Tavern

A real neighborhood dive with live music most nights. Dark, loud, genuinely local. This is where Wrigleyville residents go when they don't want game-day crowds.

Host Tip
Check the show schedule ahead. Live music is always free and the room has energy you won't find on Clark Street.
02 — Restaurants

Where we actually eat.

From pre-game bites to the deep dish you've been thinking about since you booked.

photo · Smoke Daddy BBQ / ribs
Host Pick
6 min walk $$$

Smoke Daddy BBQ

The best late-night spot on Clark Street. Smoked ribs, live blues, and a long bar. One of the few places that gets better at midnight than at dinner.

Host Tip
The pulled pork sandwich is the move. If there's live music when you walk in, stay — you won't regret it.
photo · Ann Sather cinnamon rolls
7 min walk $$$

Ann Sather

A Wrigleyville institution since 1945. Swedish-American comfort food, legendary cinnamon rolls, and weekend brunch lines that are always worth the wait.

Host Tip
Order the cinnamon rolls the moment you sit down. They come with every meal and they are what this place is famous for.
photo · Pequod's deep dish pizza
15 min rideshare $$$

Pequod's Pizza

Our pick for deep dish. The caramelized cheese crust makes it completely different from every other deep dish in the city — better, in our opinion. Worth the trip.

Host Tip
Call 30 minutes before you leave — the pizza takes 45 min to bake. Pickup is faster than delivery. Worth every minute of the wait.

Yak-Zies on Clark

A Wrigleyville sports bar that does brunch better than it has any right to. Big summer patio, easy mimosas, and always a game on. Bring a group.

04 — Things To Do

Beyond the game.

Wrigleyville is a neighborhood, not just a destination. Here's what's around you.

Gallagher Way

The plaza right next to Wrigley Field hosts free concerts, outdoor yoga, outdoor movies, winter markets, and pop-up events year-round. Check gallagherway.com before you arrive — half our guests end up at a free event the first night.

Steps from your door

Lakefront Trail

18 miles of uninterrupted path along Lake Michigan, 15 minutes east on foot. Grab a Divvy bike and ride north to Montrose Beach or south toward downtown.

15 min walk east

Wrigley Field Tour

The behind-the-scenes tour covers the Cubs dugout, press box, broadcast booths, and the ivy-covered outfield wall. A must even if you're not a baseball fan.

Across the street

Millennium Park

Cloud Gate (the Bean), Crown Fountain, the Pritzker Pavilion, and free summer concerts. Take the Red Line south — it's 15 minutes and worth the ride every time.

Red Line · 15 min south
05 — Getting Around

You don't need a car.

Seriously. The Red Line gets you everywhere and Divvy bikes cover the rest.

3 min walk

Addison Red Line

The CTA Red Line stops at Addison, three minutes from our front door. It runs 24/7 and connects directly to the Loop, Magnificent Mile, and both O'Hare and Midway airports. A 3-day Ventra pass costs $15 and covers everything.

At your door

Divvy Bikes

There's a Divvy bike-share station directly outside our building. Rent a regular or e-bike by the ride or get a day pass. The Lakefront Trail runs 18 flat miles — this is the best way to see Chicago's shoreline.

On demand

Rideshare & Taxi

Uber and Lyft work well in Wrigleyville. On game days, schedule your pickup on Roscoe Street — one block north — to skip the postgame chaos on Clark and Sheffield entirely.

A note on parking: Wrigleyville has almost no free parking, and game-day garages run $40–$60. A 3-day CTA Ventra pass costs $15 and gets you everywhere in the city. We have never once wished we'd driven here. Leave the car at home.
